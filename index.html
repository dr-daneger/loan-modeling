Monte Carlo — rate-path distribution

SOFR follows a mean-reverting (Vasicek) process; the 30-yr fixed follows a correlated one. Every draw runs through the same cap/floor reset engine, so the caps clip the upper tail — the named scenarios above are just illustrative draws from this space.

The 7-yr teaser means today's SOFR barely matters by the first reset — the spread there is close to the process's stationary distribution (std ≈ σ/√2κ around θ), not a thin cone from today. The contractual ceiling is a deep tail (needs SOFR ≥ ceiling − margin).

Refi-as-hedge guard

Refinanceability correlates with the benign world — the adverse reset tail and the “can't refi” condition arrive together. With this on, a refi is ignored in any scenario whose fixed rate at year N is at/above the threshold, so bracket C can't credit a rescue that wouldn't exist.
